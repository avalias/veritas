A market that resolves on proof, not a vote

Trading sets the price. The outcome is decided by a counting rule the contract committed to up front, applied to admitted zkTLS proofs. No token holder casts a ballot; nobody can outvote a signature.

This market's evidence window has closed and one YES proof is already admitted on chain, so it is resolvable in a single click. Resolving runs the committed rule over the admitted proofs and writes the outcome. Then the winning side redeems.

Buying YES or NO is a bet. It moves the price and lets you profit if you are right, but it has no say in how the market resolves. The outcome comes from the admitted proofs and the committed rule, full stop.

Money prices, proof decides

On UMA and similar systems, a disputed outcome is settled by a token vote: whoever holds the most tokens decides what happened. Here the resolution is a secp256k1 check inside a Move contract. To flip a YES into a NO you would have to forge a wire service's signature, which the math does not allow. Whales can move the price all day; they cannot move the verdict.

The price and the outcome are two different things. The price is a market's opinion before the fact. The outcome is what the proofs say after it. Veritas keeps them separate on purpose. That is the whole point.
