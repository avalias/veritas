Slash a lying LLM, in one on-chain step

An AI judge ran off-chain and a resolver posted its result with a bond behind it. The result was a lie. Watch the chain catch it without re-running the model, and take the resolver's money.

This is the part other verifiable-AI systems can't do cheaply. A resolver claimed a Qwen judgment produced one answer; it actually produced another. A challenger disagreed and the two played the dispute game: halve the disputed range, ask who's right, repeat. After a couple dozen rounds, 85,937 micro-operations collapse to a single one. That one operation is now small enough for a Sui contract to redo itself.

Why this beats the alternatives

Everyone can make an LLM deterministic now. The hard part is making a single wrong step cheaply provable on a public chain. Here is who pays what when a result is challenged:

SystemWho decides the disputeCost of a challenge
Veritas (this)a ~330-line Sui contract anyone can read~25 tiny txs + one min-gas micro-op
opML / ORAa public contract, but a full MIPS CPU emulatorbisect, then re-run a MIPS instruction
zkML (EZKL, Risc0)nobody, just a proofseconds-to-minutes of proving on every inference
EigenAI / TEEa staked enclave committee (≥2/3 vote)re-run the entire inference in attested TEEs
The trick is the VM speaks tensors, not assembly. Its on-chain interpreter only has to know how to redo one block dot-product, not emulate a CPU. That is why the referee fits in a few hundred lines and one micro-op costs the minimum gas bucket. The same machinery convicts the real Qwen judge (fqwen_conviction.move); here it runs a toy model so the bisection is fast to stage.
